About BBS and Syllabus

BBS (Bachelor of Business Studies) is an undergraduate program that focuses on business concepts, management skills, and the practical aspects of running a business. It is designed for students who want to pursue a career in business, management, or entrepreneurship.
The BBS program prepares students for leadership roles and equips them with the tools required for successful business ventures.

Mission of BBS Program

  1. To impart comprehensive knowledge in business and management.
  2. To develop critical thinking and problem-solving abilities in students.
  3. To prepare students for careers in business and management sectors.

Scope of BBS Program

  1. BBS graduates can work in diverse fields like finance, marketing, human resources, and operations management.
  2. Graduates can also pursue MBA or other higher education programs in business and management.

Eligibility Criteria

  1. Completion of higher secondary education (12th grade) or equivalent in any stream.
  2. Minimum marks required for admission (varies by institution).

BBS Syllabus

1st Year

  • Principles of Management
  • Business Communication
  • Financial Accounting
  • Business Mathematics
  • Microeconomics

2nd Year

  • Organizational Behavior
  • Cost Accounting
  • Macroeconomics
  • Marketing Management
  • Human Resource Management

3rd Year

  • Business Law
  • Business Ethics
  • Financial Management
  • Entrepreneurship
  • Project Work
